Ireland

Ireland is a country that I would recommend to anyone who wants to have the same opportunities as Irish people, as it is a place where discrimination toward minority groups practically doesn’t exist. Irish people are cheerful, generous, and without prejudices toward anyone.

Job opportunities in Ireland, especially in Dublin and Cork, are numerous, regardless of the skill set and occupation. There are literally tones of opportunities, especially in Dublin and Cork.

Ireland has 2 cons. The first one is that finding a house, flat, or even a room in cities like Dublin is challenging, especially if you are looking for something in the normal price range. The second one is car insurance, as quotes are one of the highest in the World.

What about Rural Ireland? The rural part of Ireland does not have a housing crisis, you can easily find a flat or even a house for less than 600 euros, but job opportunities are not the best. So, if you are looking for a better position, that is often not the best place to be unless you are working remotely.

I also heard that the healthcare system is not the best, but I never needed one when I lived there, so I’m not sure how good or bad it is.

We are happy with our life here, but we have plans to move away soon because we can’t stand the Scottish weather :D, which is worse than in any country where we lived. We’re from southern Europe, so the climate in Scotland, particularly on the North-East coast, is too much for us to deal with.

Would I recommend Ireland? Of course, it’s a fantastic country, and you can have a perfect life there. If you are a couple and both work even for minimum wage, you will never miss anything.

Why we left Ireland? Do I regret it? As I met my boyfriend there, and at that time, job opportunities and wages in his sector were pretty low in Ireland compared to the UK, we moved to the UK. As this is not the case anymore, sometimes I regret our decision. As the country where I currently live is pretty similar to Ireland in many things, I can just add I have this feeling that I always can go back to Ireland whenever I want as Ireland is culturally so close to the UK.

In any case Ireland will always have a special place in our heart, and we would always enjoy to spending some of our time there.
