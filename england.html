England

As I lived in England only for a few months, I cannot comment on life there in detail, but I can share some things I noticed. In short, England is an amazing country, but have some cons, as any country has, cons you will not notice until you live there.

England has a lot of jobs regardless of occupation, skills and experience, and very low unemployment. But, there is a reason for this, as many people work on zero-hours contracts or working part-time. Again, the same problem that Scotland has, zero-hours are legal and often here.

Rental prices in England depend on the region and the area you are looking for. Northern regions of England are far cheaper than the southern.

The NHS is really bad, waiting lists are too long, and private treatments are sometimes a few more times expensive than in Scotland. Education in England is one of the best in the World, but also one of the most expensive. Country is home of University of Oxford and University of Cambridge, as well as many other universities. If you have kids, be ready to pay thousands for childcare, as nurseries in England are unfortunately one of the most expensive in the World. So, it’s not a family-friendly country unless you are really wealthy.

In most cases, wages in England are no longer higher than in other Western countries. So, generally, in my eyes, England is one of the most unequal countries in Europe, with inadequate health care, expensive education, and childcare. The Welfare system is also one of the worst, in Western Europe. I will recommend England only to the people in occupations that pay a lot there or to the people who love to work overtime, as I do not think you can have a good life with a monthly earning under 1800£ in England.

In any case, England is a wonderful country with beautiful scenery and kind people. Land of the opportunities where you can be yourself and make your dreams a reality.
