Scotland

Scotland is a country that I would recommend to the people that have some occupation and skill set that is really in demand. Scottish people are cheerful and fun, but they are less direct than Irish about what they really think about you.

Employment prospects depend on your occupation, skills, experience and so on. Finding a nice job can be challenging unless you are in some demanding profession, especially out of Glasgow or Edinburgh. Zero-hours contracts are big problems here. They are not something positive and this type of employment is not really good for anyone else except greedy employers.

From my experience, Scotland is a great choice for people planning to work in the oil and gas sector, as Aberdeen is one of the main cities for the oil and gas industry in Europe.

Scotland has fewer job opportunities than Ireland, so there is almost no housing crisis. Finding a room/flat/house is often straightforward and pretty cheap compared to other countries in Western Europe.

The biggest problem with Scotland and the UK generally is healthcare, as the NHS is pretty bad. Honestly, I don’t know why Brits adore NHS so much, as this system is completely broken and one of the worst in Europe. Even Eastern European countries have better health systems than the UK has. You often need to pay for private treatments in the UK, as the waiting lists are too long. Childcare is also too expensive.

We are happy with our life here, but we have plans to move away soon because we can’t stand the Scottish weather :D, which is worse than in any country where we lived. We’re from southern Europe, so the climate in Scotland, particularly on the North-East coast, is too much for us to deal with.
